Eastside Promise Neighborhood
San Antonio, TX
About the Neighborhood
Eastside Promise Neighborhood (EPN) is a 2011 grantee with over 65 community partners. It is an area with more than 50 churches, many of them serving African-American populations, 296 private businesses, several social service organizations, and six schools. EPN brings together institutional and resident stakeholders to leverage and strengthen the neighborhood’s assets and resources so that children and families can thrive.
People
About 18,000 residents,
including about 5,900 public school children
Hispanic,
25% African-American
Challenges
Poverty: 60% of children living in poverty
Lack of fresh produce: Unaffordable and minimally available fresh fruits and vegetables
Resources to support college attendance: Lack of supports and minimal scholarships for college-bound students
Mobility: Families are highly transient; of the students enrolled in EPN and middle schools over the 9 years prior to baseline, none enrolled in kindergarten continued through the 8th grade
Gap in early education resources: Few affordable, high quality early childhood education programs, and few in-home books causing students to enter school with minimal reading experience
Sources: EPN 2011 Grant Application (https://www2.ed.gov/programs/promiseneighborhoods/2011narr/unitedwaysanantonio.pdf).
Place

Major strategies used
Increased time from a Peer Assistant Teacher and Reading Teacher in Pre-K classrooms
An Early Childhood Connector Corner was established on each elementary campus to provide early childhood developmental information and reading tips for young children to parents
Offered out-of-school time physical fitness programming and the EPN Get Fit Program
EPN families and their students had access to receiving fruits and vegetables when attending a school or community event and the option of participating in their local community garden and learning about healthy eating or harvesting vegetables
Intensified efforts to assist all graduating seniors in the college enrollment process, including inviting teams from local colleges to support students with the enrollment process
Selected Outcomes
Increase in kindergarteners entering school demonstrating age-appropriate functioning
Increase in children participating in at least 60 minutes of moderate to vigorous physical activity daily
Increase in children who consume five or more servings of fruits and vegetables daily
Increase in students who enrolled in college
